Independent - 1967 Pontiac GTO Restoration

Having found nothing to do in CT on co-op, I joined a coworker and spent my free time helping to restore a 1967 Pontiac GTO.

Summary

Objectives:

Whenever possible, work on the GTO to get the body attached to the frame, finish the fuel lines, get the car ready for paint.

Challenges:

  • Unlike my experience with RC cars, you cannot just adjust for the perfect angle (also everything is heavier).
  • The tools were completely new to me (air tools, ratchets, etcâ€¦). I had to learn on the spot.
  • We were not a garage, and we had to mount a body to a frame

Results:

  • GTO body panels, chrome, and frame that have been primed for painting, de-rusted, and molded with fiber glass to improve geometry
  • A mounted manual transmission and rear axle connected with driveshaft, modified frame to better fit brake and fuel lines
  • An improvised mounting mechanism using wood and jack stands to attach body to frame
  • Car finished after co-op ended, but turned out wonderfully
